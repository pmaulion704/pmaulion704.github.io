OVERVIEW
This is the main project I worked on as a UX Designer at Preqin. Preqin Pro is a web-based / platform that is geared to institutional investors, fund managers, and service providers by providing them key insights and updates on the alternative assets space. Whether you are an asset manager seeking investors to jump-start your next fund or a public pension fund looking to allocate more capital to real estate, Preqin Pro informs users about new and developing market opportunities. Aside from modernizing the look of the platform, we needed to reassess various user flows of the platform and fully incorporate a responsive layout (Desktop → Tablet) to create a more unified digital experience.
REDESIGN - SEARCH

According to user heat maps & data analytics, the quick search input field was the No. 1 (41.2%) most-clicked element on the site. The main reason for a redesign of our Search was because our original user flow contained an intermediate step which made the user select what type of profile they wanted to view according to asset class. Through user research and interviews have consistently told us that they simply want to quickly search for a specific profile in mind and immediately enter a profile view to analyze and gather data.

The first iteration of quick search focused on implementing text-string matching and incorporating a grouping system to clearly guide what the user is searching for. However, user feedback mentioned that since our grouping structure displayed vertically, search values often risked of being hidden beneath the fold.

The second iteration of quick search addressed this ‘below the fold’ issue by conglomerating all of the search results in an ‘All’ tab and can transition to a specific category with the horizontal tab structure.
